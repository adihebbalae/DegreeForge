This course introduces the fundamental concepts of electrical engineering, including
circuit analysis, signals, and basic electronics. Students will design and build simple
circuits in the laboratory and analyze them using the techniques developed in lecture.

Required Text and Equipment:
F. T. Ulaby and M. M. Maharbiz, Circuit Analysis and Design, 1st edition (Michigan Publishing, 2018).
Supplementary Reference:
C. K. Alexander and M. N. O. Sadiku, Fundamentals of Electric Circuits, 7th edition (McGraw-Hill, 2021).

Note: discussion of homework problems is encouraged, but every student must work independently.

Grading:
Homework    20%
Quizzes     10%
Two Midterm Exams   40%
Final Exam   30%

Course Outline
Week 1   Introduction and basic concepts
Week 2   Kirchhoff's laws and circuit topology
Week 3   Nodal and mesh analysis
Week 4   Thevenin and Norton equivalents
